Do You Need a Nudge?

Several years ago, I read a business book entitled "Nudge" by Thaler and Sunstein. Informative read and it underscored some inconvenient facts about human nature. Yes, we tend to procrastinate and worse, we tend to not get around to doing the things that we want to do and know we should do.

The adage "Not to decide is to decide" is useful to remember. How often have we said to ourselves, I meant to do that sooner. The scout motto is Be Prepared. Being prepared means, among other things, taking care of important matters in a timely fashion.

Log onto my.scouting.org and review your training history and complete any open items. Check your Youth Protection, and Position training. Everyone needs Hazardous Weather Training. Set up a time in your calendar to complete these items.

Roundtables are a great resource and it is easy to put this off as well, so resolve to go to Roundtable.

We all know we want to support scouting, both directly with our own contributions of time and treasure, but we also need to help in other ways like scheduling FOS presentations for your unit and promoting council fund raising activities like Popcorn and Adventure Card sales. These are items which we know we should do, but it‚Äôs easy to push them aside for later and later never comes.

Youth Protection Training (YPT)

Avoid the rush, check your card and get it done. Training is valid for two years and you must retake the online course. If you are out of date, it is full stop. Go to my.scouting.org for this and other training opportunities. Don't forget Hazardous Weather Training and Range Activities Safety Training.
