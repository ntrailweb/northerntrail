Talent Stack

In the business world we see a constant stream of books with the next great concept for success and a recent one is "building a talent stack." Not really a new idea, just a different twist. The point is that most of us will never reach the pinnacle of performance in any one skill, however the combination of skills can make a huge difference in our individual success. Scouting is a terrific way to build a talent stack. Beginning with the scout oath and law our scouts internalize from the very beginning the importance of these qualities and responsibilities. Scouting then exposes our scouts to the outdoors and takes them out of their comfort zone and challenges them to adapt to new situations and solve problems. Through the advancement process our scouts are tested and must demonstrate proficiency in a number of vital life skills from first aid to financial literacy. The merit badge program with the essential required set and a smorgasbord of topics covering just about any profession, gives scouts the opportunity to experience a taste of a future career.

A critical skill scouts learn is both how to lead and work effectively with leaders, both good and bad. Leadership is hard and being exposed to the challenges of leadership under difficult conditions is wonderful preparation for life.

Scouting is much more than simple entertainment; it is supplemental education in vital life skills.

Re-Charter

The re-charter process is now underway, and you can begin the process. Every unit must complete the re-charter process at this time each year.

There are 3 scheduled recharter clinics set, all at FUMC-Richardson all starting at 7:00 pm. The dates are November 11, November 30, and December 9.

If you need additional help, please contact your unit commissioner or Hank Voegtle who can be reached at: HJV3@outlook.com

Every year many units must scramble at the last minute to complete this important task. This makes it hard on everyone involved in process, please get started today. If you are not directly involved in the re-charter, you still need to get your dues paid and complete youth protection training if you have an expiring YPT card.

It is Time to Recognize Adult Scouters

Scouting only exists due the tireless work of volunteers and the unit, district, council and national level. We are indebted to these individuals who are critical to the success of the scouting movement.

We have several awards to recognize these individuals beginning at the unit level with the Your Extraordinary Service Award or the Y.E.S. Award. At the district level we have two awards: the District Award of Merit and the Robert E. Little Award. Applications for these awards must be submitted no later than Friday, December 10th. No applications will be accepted after this date.

At the Council level we have the Silver Beaver Award. Applications for the Silver Beaver Award must be received no later than Friday, November 12th. No applications will be accepted after this date.

Self-nominations will not be accepted.

More information, including the submission details of these awards, is available here.

For any questions, please contact the Awards and Recognition Chair, Muhammad Ikram at mzi@gatech.edu.
