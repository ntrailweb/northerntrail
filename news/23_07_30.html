Recruiting

Recruiting is a year-round process, but most recruiting occurs in the fall. School nights for scouting are coming soon and history shows that when youth are exposed to scouting, they are enthusiastic. The more difficult part is selling the parents on the benefits of scouting. Keep in mind that scouting is not about entertainment, the fun is the hook and the goals are embodied in the AIMs of scouting codified in the scout oath and law.

Once you have made the "sale" it is vital to follow through and make new scouts and their parents feel welcome and become an active part of your unit.

Many join in the beginning of the year, but fail to continue in the program. This is where an active program of follow-though pays off. Plan on contacting the no-shows and work to keep them engaged in scouting. Just a little bit of effort can make a huge difference in your retention of new members. Don't leave it to chance, have a plan in place and work it.

Wood Badge 142

Wood Badge is the Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training designed to meet the advanced leadership needs of Scouters.

Wood Badge brings together leaders from all areas of Scouting - Cub Scouting, Scouts BSA and Venturing. Our mission is to inspire and train adults through world class leadership training that sets an example for youth empowerment.

Wood Badge 142 will be held at Camp Constantin the weekends of October 20-22, 2023 and November 4-5, 2023. Registration deadline October 10, 2023.
