BALOO Training

Baloo Training (Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation) is the Cub Scout leader training required for any Cub Scout den or pack outdoor event, including pack camping overnighters and Webelos den overnighters. This training provides you with the knowledge that you need to know in order to successfully hold these outdoor events. Registration: https://scoutingevent.com/571-2021NTSpringBALOO

Scouts BSA Leadership Training

Leaders and parents. This training is for you. Adult Leadership Training (ALT) is a two evening classroom program which teaches you the details of how a scout unit operates by taking on the role of a scout in a unit. Learn by doing! Spring NYLT

National Youth Leadership Training in 2021. First course will be during spring break: March 3-14, 2021. NYLT is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. More information.

ILST

April 10-11, 2021 at Camp Wisdom. The purpose of the ILST (Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops) course is to teach Scouts with leadership positions about their new roles and how to most effectively reach success in that role. It is intended to help Scouts in leadership positions within their troop understand their responsibilities and to equip them with organizational and leadership skills to fulfill those responsibilities. 2021 Friends of Scouting Campaign

We are collectively facing the most challenging time in recent history, and COVID has profoundly impacted all aspects of our lives. While the virus has disrupted many activities, the scouting program has continued and is adapting to the challenges of meeting in groups while keeping Scouts and leaders safe through social distancing, masks and plenty of hand washing and hand sanitizer. As scouts and adults have increased safety measures to keep everyone healthy, we are asking you to help us keep the financial foundation of scouting solid by donating generously to Friends of Scouting this year. We are asking everyone to step up their financial support to ensure the continued economic stability of all aspects of the program. Even in the midst of a pandemic, we are hoping you will consider playing a part in the council’s continued ability to maintain top notch facilities and offer outstanding programs for our scouting youth.

You can help us reach this year’s Friends of Scouting goal of $150,000 for our district through a variety of avenues. The simplest way to donate TODAY is to utilize the online link we have provided here. You can also help us attain this ambitious goal by approaching your employer/corporation to see if they offer a matching gift program and/or will pay for volunteer hours (more to come about this possibility in a future newsletter). Finally, we encourage you to reach out to Vaughn Dubose, our FOS Family Chairperson, to schedule one of our FOS speakers to make a presentation at one of your unit meetings, at your Blue and Gold dinner, or at a Court of Honor. Vaughn can be reached via email at vaughndubose@gmail.com.

If you have questions, please contact Trey Otte, our 2021 Friends of Scouting Chairperson, at trey@otte.us.

A Celebration of Scouting

Please join us at Circle Ten Council's Annual Recognition Banquet at the Plano Event Center (2000 E. Spring Creek Parkway, Plano) on Thursday, March 25, 2021 at 6:30 p.m.
