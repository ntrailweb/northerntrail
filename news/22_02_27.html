Volunteer or Voluntold

Scouting relies on the work of thousands of volunteers to deliver the program to our youth. Den Leaders, Cub Masters, Scoutmasters, Merit Badge Counselors and committee members are all examples of opportunities to help.

There is an old joke in scouting, "it's only an hour a week." In addition to taking more time, it also takes more than one individual leading the program.

There are so many opportunities to help. Volunteers are needed to drive to campouts, bake cookies for Blue & Gold banquets, serve as Walking Den Leaders for Twilight Camp, Popcorn Kernel or Camp Card Coordinator to name just a few.

No one likes it, but it is often necessary to resort to voluntold. How many times have you been at a meeting and heard the call for volunteers and no one steps up? Those leaders who do step up can't carry the load all by themselves. A hardworking leader put in this position will be discouraged by the silence. You don’t want discouraged leaders, discouraged leaders will fade away. Volunteering in scouting is very rewarding and those of us who have done this a long time can tell you that it is satisfying, and we can see the difference we are making. However, we all get tired and discouraged when we don't have enough help. Everyone has their obligations and limitations on time. We cannot have success without your help as the saying goes, "Many hands make light work." The next time you hear a call for help, please don't hesitate to step up and if you are voluntold, please cheerfully say "yes I will help."

Changes To Requirements 2021/2022

A number of merit badges had changes in their requirements last year and others are changing this year. A Scout who started a merit badge using the “prior” requirements may continue with those requirements; while Scouts beginning a merit badge should use the most current set of requirements. For more information see Guide to Advancement, Section 7.0.4.3, What to do When Requirements Change ...filestore/pdf/33088.pdf. The 2021 changes are currently reflected in Scoutbook while the 2022 changes will be posted shortly. Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops

ILST (Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops) is scheduled for April 30-May 1, 2022, at Camp Wisdom. This is for any Scout with a leadership position on the Patrol Leader Council. The course teaches troop organization, leadership skills, how to teach using EDGE and is designed to teach by using games, reflections and other methods to engage youth. Scout's Own is included in the course to be a guide to scouts who serve as Chaplain's aide. Reference material for Scouts is the Patrol Leader's Handbook. On-line Registration. Contact Bill Wilson if you have questions. wrw@billwilsonlaw.com

Who: Youth desiring to be trained in the Leadership Skills required for their positions.
What: The purpose of the ILST course is to teach Scouts with leadership positions about their new roles and how to most effectively reach success in that role. ILST is the first course in the series of leadership training offered to Scouts and is a replacement for Troop Leadership Training. This next course in the series is National Youth Leadership Training.
When: April 30-May 2-2022. Arrive at 8:00 A .M. on April 30 2022. Dismiss at 11:00 AM on May 1, 2022.
Where: Camp Wisdom
