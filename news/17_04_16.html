Limits to Growth

Some of you may be aware that the number of scouts in our district has declined since last year at this time. We hope to make up this shortfall with some active recruiting, but what are the underlying reasons?

While other activities are a factor, the real problem is the lack of adult volunteers.

Scouting has very few paid professionals. The real boots on the ground come from volunteers like you within the units. Committee members, scout masters, cub masters, den leaders and crew advisors are all volunteers.

The backbone of the organization is the unit committee, made up of volunteers like you who help make it happen. The committee chair has the critical task of recruiting volunteers and coordinating activities of the committee.

The reason the committee exists, is to make good use of the talent available and spread the load so no one person is burdened with all of the work.

So when your committee chairman or unit leaders asks for help, please step up to the plate and get involved. Even a small amount of effort makes a difference. The program will suffer without adequate help. Units which drop are usually due to a lack of willing adult leaders, there are plenty of youth who want to participate in the program.

So next time you hear a call for help in your unit, step up and help make it a success. Remember the life of the unit and service to future scouts depend on your efforts.

Twilight Camp June 5-9, 2017


Twilight Camp would not be possible without parents and grandparents who volunteer to help with the event. These Waling Den Leaders attend camp and walk with the scouts from station to station. The challenge for the organizers is to have enough den leaders each day. Training, including Face to Face Youth Protection for walking den leaders will be held in May.
