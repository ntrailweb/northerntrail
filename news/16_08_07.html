Roundtable Schedule Change

Roundtable will be held this "TUESDAY August 9th at 7:00PM." This is a change in schedule from prior years. Going forward Roundtable will be held at the First United Methodist Church of Richardson, 503 N Central Expressway Room 212 in the Education Building. Map Roundtable is for all leaders and parents in all programs. Training, tips and information about all aspects of scouting is available. The Order of the Arrow meeting is held at the same location and time in Room 211.

The Boy Scout Roundtable this month will focus on "How To Do a Board of Review". District Advancement Chair, Bob Faulkner, is our guest speaker. The presentation and discussion are designed to help new parents and new leaders learn the process of a Board of Review. The boys have worked on rank advancements all summer. Now they're ready for their reviews. Often times, adults are reluctant to help with them because they do not know what to do. The shortage of adults willing to participate results in advancement delays and the boys to becoming discouraged. This introduction to the process will encourage everyone to be excited about volunteering. If you're new to Boy Scouts, been around a few years, or your one of the "old guys", your attendance is helpful and greatly appreciated.

Popcorn

REGISTER FOR POPCORN KERNEL TRAINING TODAY!!! We have three more training sessions for Popcorn Kernels on Aug. 9, 11 and 13. You will need to register for and attend one of these “batting practices” this week. Before you can attend a training session, you must first register your pack/troop/team for popcorn sales. Visit the Council website to sign up, for additional details and for critical dates at circleten.org/popcorn.

In addition, you won’t want to miss out on an opportunity to attend the exciting and motivating Michael Beck Sales Training, to be held at AMC Valley View 16 on Thursday, August 18. Selling popcorn is easy with the right training and support, and achieving high popcorn sales goals will help fund both your unit operations and the Council’s programming. Selling popcorn also teaches Scouts the value of contributing their time and effort to making the Scouting program the best it can be. Register TODAY!

If you have any questions about popcorn sales or training, please contact your District Popcorn Kernels Patti and Trey Otte at 214.405.4718 (Patti) or 214.402.4718 (Trey). We are looking forward to working with you.

Second Annual Outdoor Adventure

Mark your Calendar; Saturday September 10, 2016, Yale Park, Map

Help us kick off our Fall recruiting by participating in the 2nd Annual Outdoor Adventure community event!
