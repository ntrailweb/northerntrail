🌞 SUMMER THOUGHTS FROM THE TRAIL 🌞
Be the Scout Someone Remembers
Summer camp is full of memorable moments. Scouts remember the campfires, the friendships, the canoe races, the hikes, and sometimes even the food.

But years later, many Scouts remember something else even more clearly: a person who helped them.

It might have been an older Scout who showed them how to set up a tent, a patrol leader who made them feel welcome, or an adult leader who encouraged them to try something they didn't think they could do.

This summer, challenge yourself to be that person for someone else. Invite the new Scout to join your table. Help carry gear without being asked. Encourage a friend who is struggling with a merit badge or homesickness. Teach a skill. Share a laugh.

Big adventures make camp exciting, but small acts of kindness are often what make camp unforgettable.

At the end of the week, merit badges will be recorded, patches will be sewn on uniforms, and gear will be unpacked. What will remain longest are the memories we create and the people whose lives we touched along the way.

Make this the summer that someone remembers because of you.

Northern Trail has an active commissioner staff with deep scouting knowledge. Get to know your commissioner.
