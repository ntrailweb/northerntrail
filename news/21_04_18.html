Twilight Camp 2021, June 14-18, 2021

Twilight camp is a week-long late afternoon, early evening event for Cub Scouts. The theme for Twilight Camp this year is "Weird Science." Lots of fun activities are planned in a COVID safe environment. This event will be held, as in the past, at Breckinridge Park in Northeast Richardson. BB guns, archery, crafts, games, egg drop, swaps and more. Registration now available. Check out pictures from past Twilight Camps here. Another benefit is that as an adult, you will enjoy the activity and meet other parents dealing with the many of the same challenges you are facing.

On-Line Pack/Troop Committee Member Training

Pack Committee member training requires only 53 minutes of your time and Troop Committee member training is 102 minutes. Both provide critical information about the role of the committee in unit operations. A well-run committee will help your unit run more effectively and efficiently. Check out this and other training opportunities at my.scouting.org.

National Youth Leadership Training

National Youth Leadership Training is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. Learn more.

Friends of Scouting

It has been a really tough year for everyone. Some more so than others. All of us have suffered due to lack of social interactions with friends and colleagues. Scouting has suffered, it has been more difficult to deliver the program. Everyone is adapting, however Circle Ten has suffered financially as well. It takes money to care for our camps, offices and pay the dedicated professionals who work hard to create the environment needed to deliver the program. Many of us have stepped up and dug a little deeper to fill the gap, but we are still short of what is needed to keep the council financially healthy.

If you can help, please consider making a pledge or an additional pledge. You can do it online.
