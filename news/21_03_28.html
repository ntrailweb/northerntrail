About Northern Trail District

Circle Ten Council is divided into districts, each of which cover a specific geographical area. Northern Trail district covers the same area as the Richardson Independent School district. This includes most of Richardson and parts of Garland, Dallas and Plano. There is a team of volunteers in place at the district level who are there to help make scouting a success. Each unit is assigned a unit commissioner who is an experienced scouting leader who can answer questions and help you make your unit successful. More information.

Training for Adults

Scouting is a unique concept and the aims and methods are not immediately obvious. It can be a little overwhelming to someone new. This problem has been addressed and there is a wealth of training available to help ensure the success or your child and unit.

Online training is available at my.scouting.org. Just sign up for an account and start training.

Here is an in-person opportunity coming up in the district:

BALOO Training Saturday-Sunday April 10-11, 2021.

Northern Trail and Tejas Caddo will be offering BALOO training April 10-11, 2021 at Camp Wisdom. BALOO is Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation and is required by BSA for any Cub Scout den or pack outdoor event, including pack camping overnighters and Webelos den overnighters. In this course you will learn:

  • How to use GPS for Geocaching.
  • Safely hold Aquatics programs.
  • How to select gear for campouts.
  • Basics of hiking and how to make it fun.
  • How to Leave No Trace.
  • Campout cooking and sanitary practices for cooking and cleaning.
  • Basic first aid.
  • How to use and care for pocketknives.
  • How to light a stove and lantern and fire safety.
  • How to tie basic knots.

Cost of course is $30.00 and since we teach the Buddy System in Scouts, we are offering the "bring a buddy" discount of 2 for $50.00

50th Anniversary Environmental Protection Agency Award

The Environmental Protection Agency, as part of a public awareness campaign to celebrate its first 50 years, has partnered with the Boy Scouts of America to create an award for members of troops, crews and ships. This new award will recognize youth who research and learn about the subjects of human health and the environment, as the EPA develops its vision for the next 50 years. Fund Raising

Like it or not, any organization requires funds to make it operate. Scouting is a very efficent operation with volunteer leaders who do the heavy lifting. We have camps, offices and professional scouters who provide essential services. These professionals represent a small fraction of the total. Funds are raised in several ways including fees, popcorn sales, camp cards and direct contributions from individuals and groups. All of these income sources have declined due to COVID has this has placed a severe strain on the organization and it is vital that we make an extra effort to get back on track.
