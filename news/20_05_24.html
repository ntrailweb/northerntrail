Twilight Camp

Since Twilight Camp has been restructured due to COVID-19, the “at Home” Camp Experience is now in progress and activities can be downloaded at https://circleten.ihubapp.org/posts/54130/handpickedfun. The general idea is for you and your Cub Scout to spend 2 hours/day for 5 days creating and enjoying your own "at-Home" Cub Scout Day Camp. No, you don't have to do 5 days in a row! Do it when it works for your family and/or use the guide all summer long!

We know the things Cub Scouts love BEST about Day Camp are some of the things you can't do at home, such as archery and BB guns! That is why Circle Ten Council has also designed an "at-Camp" Experience as well. Over a variety of days in June and August, a parent/Scout can register for a Cub Scout day camp at one of the Circle Ten Council camps. This is a family event, and while a parent can bring all their children (Scouts and siblings), no Scout may attend without a parent. This "at-Camp" experience will include archery, BB guns, swimming, games, crafts, and lunch. More details will be forthcoming, including how to register!

Volunteers are needed to staff these activity areas: games, crafts, and cleaning crews. If you have the appropriate certifications, there might be openings for you in the archery or BB guns areas. There will be two cleaning crews, one on the days of camp to clean and disinfect between classes and the other on the day after camp to give everything a deep cleaning. If you can help on one or more days, and we really do need the help, please contact Hank Voegtle at 214-769-5040.

Merit Badges from Home

At Least 58 merit badges that Scouts can complete from home. The merit badges on the list meet two main criteria:

  • They don’t involve visits to public places or companies.
  • Their requirements can be completed indoors or in an average-size yard.
Here is the link to the article on Bryan for Scouting. https://blog.scoutingmagazine.org/2020/03/20/merit-badges-for-social-distancing/.

During this challenging time, we sometimes find it difficult for Scouts to meet certain requirements. We can be tempted to apply "common sense" in interpreting those requirements But one fundamental of the merit badge program is that requirements must be met as written and no one, or no authority, can change those requirements, unless they are officially changed at BSA.

Several merit badges have requirements that a Scout "attend" a meeting or some other function. Our Council Advancement Chair has recently weighed in on this one. The dictionary meaning of "attend" is "be present at". That means the Scout needs to be present in person. Given that many meetings these days are on-line, if a Scout is present via Zoom, or some similar tool, as the meeting or function is happening, then that does meet the definition of "attend". However, watching a recording or time-delayed video stream does not meet the definition.

This means that certain merit badge requirements, including some Eagle required merit badges such as Citizenship in the Community, cannot be completed until the Scout can attend in person, or the meeting is conducted and available on some real-time, on-line platform. There are plenty of other merit badges that can be earned, and most requirements can be met while still maintaining the mandated social distancing.

Philmont Wood Badge Course August 17 - 22, 2020

Get signed up today to attend this course at Philmont this summer!
Wood Badge is Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training for adults; designed for leaders of all BSA programs to help show those leaders how to ensure their youth get the most out of the Scouting program. Wood Badge consists of two parts. The first is a five-day practical program, usually offered in two 3-day camping experiences. The second part, completed over the following 18 months, requires participants to apply the skills they have learned in their local Scouting program by completing five goals (tickets) leading towards the participant's vision.
