Dates in Calendar are Closer Than They Appear

Wow, it's already July. It won't be long before school starts. First day of school for RISD is August 16th. Now is the time to get ready for your fall programs, campouts and adult leader training. It will also be time to get ready for re-charter.

From experience I can tell you that re-charter is relatively painless if you start early. Everyone in a unit has a role. If you are responsible for re-charter, it's imperative that you get the word out to your unit early and often that it's time to complete the required training and get any required paperwork turned in. Everyone else needs to take this task seriously and get it done well before the deadline. Waiting until the last minute is a recipe for frustration. Start now and get the lion's share done early and then it's not too difficult to wrap it up at the end.

Remember every registered adult, must have a current Youth Protection Card to complete the re-charter process. YPT is good for two years. It just takes some time in front of a computer to complete the course. So check your records and remind folks who need training to get it done now.

Speaking of training. Every front line leader needs to be trained for the position they hold. Both on-line and and preferably in-person training is available. Watch for details for in-person training in the fall.

Training

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Scholarship Opportunity

The FBI Dallas Citizen Academy Alumni Association continues its tradition of providing two $1,000 scholarships to support those continuing their education at a college and/or university in the study field of law enforcement.

They are offered to current high school seniors, college freshman, or college sophomores, who are in good academic standing, demonstrate leadership, community service, are of good character, and interested in pursuing a career in law enforcement/criminal justice. Each $1,000 scholarship will be awarded in two installments of $500.

Qualifications

US citizenship, State of Texas resident, High school senior, college freshman or college sophomore, 3.0 grade point average or higher, attending or planning to attend a 2- or 4-year college/university in the Fall of 2022, dependents of FBI employees are not eligible to apply.

Feel free to share with anyone you know who might be interested. The deadline is Friday, July 22, 2022. Please contact your Director-Education, Sammy Yang at syang@fbidallascaaa.org with any questions.

Link to application: https://www.fbidallascaaa.org/scholarship-program-2022/
