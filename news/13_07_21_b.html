Commissioners Corner

Time for Plan for Scout Year

Planning is a critical part of all of our lives.   While we can't always predict what will happen, a good plan is a recipe for success.   Since things don't always go as we would expect or like, it's prudent to consider alternatives well ahead of time so you can quickly adapt.   If you are a parent or leader, it's time to consider the upcoming events in the fall and be ready to participate.  

There are a number of district events including Northern Trail to Adventure, Join Scouting Night, Popcorn Sale, Webelos Woods, Oak Leaf, Camp Cards, Adult Award Nominations, Annual Dinner and Camporeewhich will be here soon.   As a participant it's important to reserve the date and as an event lead, to get your team together and plan the details of the activity.  One of those details is getting the word out about the events.  

Another important set of coming activities are the many training opportunities.   The scout program is unique and the aims and methods of the program are not obvious.  Participating in a training session will make you a better leader, provide you with the knowledge to help your own child get more out of the program and it's great fun.   Training is a great way to meet other parents who are facing the similar challenges.  Oh, and did I mention, it's fun.  

In addition to training, we have monthly roundtables.   Every unit should be represented at roundtable.  Roundtables are a combination of training, information and camaraderie.  Oh, and did I mention, it’s fun!

The webmaster can help with promotion of events on the website and the newsletter, but he needs help in the form of timely information. Providing the information in exactly the way you want it to appear, really makes the process much easier.     

Submitted by John Slaughter
