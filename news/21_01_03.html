Sunday January 3, 2021

Happy New Year

While 2020 is behind us, unfortunately some of the unique challenges are still with us and will continue for some time. In addition, there will be new challenges yet to face. As I said last week, the scouting program is designed to help equip scouts to face life challenges we know about and the ones yet to come. A scout is cheerful; a cheerful attitude will accomplish far more than lamenting how bad things are. A scout is helpful, and a willingness to step up and help, is a recipe for better results.

The new year is often a time to make resolutions. Let's resolve to meet the challenges head on and move forward. If your unit has been meeting rarely or worse yet has a delinquent charter, change that. Schedule a zoom meeting or a socially distanced event in a local park and talk about how to get your unit energized again. Review the recommendations and plan some safe events like campouts and meetings based on the best information available. Don't let the virus win! We need scouting skills more than ever.

Finally, it's no secret that many of us had financial impacts as a result of the pandemic, this has put severe strain on many. Circle Ten Council is feeling the effect as well. Please consider how your unit can help with the Friends of Scouting drive and the camp card program. We've lost some of the momentum and it's time to push a little harder and catch up. Even small amounts can make a difference. Scouting works, scouting matters.

Spring NYLT

National Youth Leadership Training in 2021. First course will be during spring break: March 3-14, 2021. NYLT is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. More information.
