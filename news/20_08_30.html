EXPIRED Youth Protection Cards

We still have many volunteers and leaders with recently expired YPT cards. YPT is designed to protect not only our scouts, but adult volunteers. It's vital that everyone understand the critical concepts and procedures which keep everyone safe. Just because someone might not be interacting in person, does not change the requirement that all adults have a valid current YPT card. Please take the time now to get current. Go to my.scouting.org/ to take the training. Only takes about an hour.

BSA Bankruptcy and Sexual Abuse

We know Scouting is a safe place for our youth. But in the upcoming weeks there will be more and more ads from attorneys soliciting victims of sexual abuse to make claims for damages. There are two reasons for this, first the Bankruptcy court has set a deadline for these claims to be filed, and second because states have lifted their statutes of limitations for this type of claim. The targets are men who were scouts in the 50s, 60s, 70s, and 80s. If asked about the bankruptcy let people know it is the National Council and does directly not affect Circle Ten and let them know about the changes that have been made in youth protection. Scouting is the best program for positive character development.

Wood Badge Courses

Get signed up today to attend a Wood Badge Course!
Wood Badge is Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training for adults; designed for leaders of all BSA programs to help show those leaders how to ensure their youth get the most out of the Scouting program. Wood Badge consists of two parts. The first is a five-day practical program, usually offered in two 3-day camping experiences. The second part, completed over the following 18 months, requires participants to apply the skills they have learned in their local Scouting program by completing five goals (tickets) leading towards the participant's vision.
