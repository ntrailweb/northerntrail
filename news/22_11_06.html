Physically Fit

Every time our scouts recite the scout oath, one of the tenets is "Physically Fit." What does this really mean? A common definition is; "A set of attributes that people have or achieve that relates to the ability to perform physical activity." Participation in outdoor activities, hiking and sports are a path to fitness. A healthy diet, getting enough sleep and shedding excess pounds are major factors. As adults, more than anything else, we lead by our example. Scouts notice what we do, more than what we say. It's no secret that most of us carry too much weight and have less than healthy eating habits. There are things you can do. Our troop for example, did not allow any carbonated or sugary beverages on camp-outs. In the beginning we got a lot of grumbling from the scouts, but after a while they got the message. We always had lots of water to drink! In addition, we did our best to have camp-outs which required the scouts to bring their backpacks and carry their gear to a campsite a good distance from the cars. This had two benefits, they got some good exercise and they learned to bring less stuff!

There are no easy answers, but helping our scouts get and stay fit early in life will make it easier for them to develop lifelong good habits of regular exercise and a good diet.

So instead of just having your scouts simply recite this tenet of the scout oath, give your scouts the tools to achieve fitness through your example and guidance.

Training

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Watch the calendar and newsletter for information about available training.

Annual Re-Charter

Every unit must re-charter their unit each year. This involves several steps. You can start working on this now.

  • Update rosters both youth and adult.
  • Review training status for every adult. Current Youth Protection is required.
  • Collect adult and scout applications if required.
  • Collect fees

Start now and this process will be much easier. Wait until the last minute and it is hard on everyone. Help is available, just ask!
