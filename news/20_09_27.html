Youth Protection Training is Required

There have been some problems reported with my.scouting.org. As of right now the system is working. Sometimes it gets busy. If you have forgotten your user ID and or password, there is a recovery process you can go through. This works even if your email has changed. Just be sure and have your BSA id number.

Many in Northern Trail lack current YPT cards. Two years ago a new system was implemented and everyone who took the training then will need to take it again. As an adult you cannot participate in any scouting activity in-person or on-line unless you have a current Youth Protection Card. These expire every two years and because of changes two years ago, most of us need new cards. This includes Venturing scouts who are 18 years of age or older.

Your unit re-charter cannot be completed until every member has a current card. Avoid the rush, get it done today.

YPT is designed to protect not only our scouts, but adult volunteers. It's vital that everyone understand the critical concepts and procedures which keep everyone safe. Just because someone might not be interacting in person, does not change the requirement that all adults have a valid current YPT card. Please take the time now to get current. Go to my.scouting.org/ to take the training. Only takes about an hour.

ALT / IOLS Training

Training starts on Wednesday of this week via Zoom. This training is for Leaders in the ScoutsBSA program and is targeted at leaders and parents who want to learn the details of how a troop operates. Emphasis is placed on Scout-Led activities and how to manage troop meetings and campouts. You learn by taking on the role of a scout and learn the ropes just as the scouts do in an actual unit. It's a fun rewarding activity and you will meet other scouting adults who are facing the same challenges. The Adult Leader Specific or ALT portion of the program will be done using Zoom and meets twice. The first meeting is Wednesday, September 30th at 7:00PM and the second session is the following Wednesday October 7th at 7:00PM.

Introduction to Outdoor Leaders Skills is an actual scout campout over the weekend of October 17th and 18th at Camp Wisdom. The campout planning will be a part of the ALT training. To be considered "trained" you need to complete both ALT and the IOLS.

Registration is required. Registration now available.

Revised COVID-19 Rules for Advancement

Extension of Time for Eagle Rank - Earlier this year, National allowed councils to grant a 3 month extension to complete the Eagle requirements when inability to meet the requirements by the 18th birthday was caused by the COVID-19 epidemic. NEW: National has now allowed Councils to grant an additional 3 month extension under the same guidelines. This authority is currently set to expire on Dec 31, 2020.

Swimming - Second Class (5b) and First Class (6a) swimming requirements may be deferred until each Scout’s next rank, as necessary (through Life, but not Eagle). In other words. a scout may advance up to Life by deferring these requirements until the Scout's first advancement after National reinstates these requirements. For that next advancement, the scout must pass any of these deferred requirements.

Camping Merit Badge - Requirement 9b is temporarily suspended (not deferred) until National restores it after the COVID-19 emergency. All other requirements, including camping nights, must be met to earn the Camping MB.

Can Merit Badge Requirements be Adapted - Leaders and merit badge counselors must not make additions or deletions to requirements. The Scout is expected to meet the requirements as stated; in some cases, virtual “visits” may fulfill the intent of a requirement. When the requirement’s intent cannot be reached virtually, the requirements cannot be completed, and the Scout must wait to complete that badge/requirement. Merit badge counselors signing off on requirements must determine to the best of their ability if the Scout has demonstrated the intended outcome.

To request this extension, email the following information to the District Advancement Chair, Bob Faulkner b.c.faulkner@att.net

  • Scout's Name, Birthday, Unit #, and BSA PID#.
  • A current copy of the scout's advancement report.
  • The name, phone # & Email of the Unit Leader and a parent
  • An overview of the situation (what caused the delay)

Ref: https://www.scouting.org/coronavirus/covid-19-faq/ Contact Bob Faulkner b.c.faulkner@att.net if you have questions.

Wood Badge Courses

Get signed up today to attend a Wood Badge Course!
Wood Badge is Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training for adults; designed for leaders of all BSA programs to help show those leaders how to ensure their youth get the most out of the Scouting program. Wood Badge consists of two parts. The first is a five-day practical program, usually offered in two 3-day camping experiences. The second part, completed over the following 18 months, requires participants to apply the skills they have learned in their local Scouting program by completing five goals (tickets) leading towards the participant's vision.
