🔥 Quick-Thinking Cub Scout Saves the Day! 🔥

Just in case you missed all the excitement in last week's newsletter -

Two weekends ago, Cub Scout Layne was out crawfish hunting in the creek with his dad when adventure turned real. A fire had broken out on a neighbor’s patio.

While his dad ran to alert the neighbors, Layne didn’t hesitate — he grabbed a bucket, filled it with creek water, and went to work. It took several trips (and even a tumble into the creek!), but his quick thinking stopped the fire from spreading to the upstairs neighbors.

That’s what being a Scout is all about — helpful, brave, and prepared to act when it matters most.

Layne is being recognized October 9 by the Richardson ISD Board of Trustees with the “Be The One” Award, which is given to staff or students who go above and beyond their roles by demonstrating leadership or exceptional effort within the RISD community.

👏 Way to go, Layne! You’re a true example of Scout spirit in action.

Take Course

The new course reinforces a culture of active supervision, situational awareness, and safety. S.A.F.E. guarding Youth is 90 minutes the first time you take it, then only 15-minute annual refreshers.

2025 Fall Leave No Trace/Outdoor Ethics Certification Trainer Class

As an LNT Trainer you can:

  • Host effective LNT Awareness Events for your Unit or District
  • Assist in facilitating LNT Trainer's course
  • Improve your presentation skills
  • Take control of and greatly improve your unit's outdoor skills and experiences
    • Note a partcipant must attend BOTH the indoor and outdoor portions for his/her class

By registering for the Indoor Session, you are automatically registered for the Outdoor Session. Both are required to earn the Leave No Trace Trainer Certification.
