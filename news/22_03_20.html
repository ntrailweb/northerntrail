Attend Summer Camp

Summer camp is an essential component of your scouts development. I have wonderful vivid memories of my summer scout camp experiences which occurred decades ago. For many scouts, summer camp is the first time away from home for an extended length of time. This is an opportunity to learn some life skills in dealing with new situations and different support structures. Camp also provides the opportunity to earn merit badges and have some great fun at the same time. If you troop is not going to summer camp, your scout can still participate. The Maverick scout program makes this possible.

Maverick Camp at Circle Ten Summer Camps

Did you know that Scouts can attend Circle Ten Summer Camp without your whole troop going?

Whether a Scout is wanting more summer camp than they can get with your troop, need a few more merit badges, their schedule conflicts with your summer camp offerings, or parents are not comfortable sending them far away, a Scout can attend any session of Summer Camp at either Trevor Rees-Jones Scout Camp or Camp Constantin/Jack D Furst Aquatic’s Base. They will camp together with other Scouts in the same situation and have staff assigned to serve as their Scoutmaster’s for the week.

To register, visit the camp registration page below and register them as a Maverick. If an adult would like to go with their scout, they can register as well.

Training 2022

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Time to sign up for BALOO

BALOO is Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation. BSA requires 2 BALOO trained adults for each Cub Scout campout. This course is for all levels of Adult Camping experience. We teach the basics needed for you and your scouts to be comfortable in the Outdoors. Topics include, selecting gear, how to light a stove and cook camp meals, knot tying , water safety, basic first aid, fun games for campouts and how to plan a campfire. Come on down to Camp Wisdom April 30 for a fun, informative weekend. Black pug sign up link: https://scoutingevent.com/571-2022NTSEWHBALOO

Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops

ILST (Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops) is scheduled for April 30-May 1, 2022, at Camp Wisdom. This is for any Scout with a leadership position on the Patrol Leader Council. The course teaches troop organization, leadership skills, how to teach using EDGE and is designed to teach by using games, reflections and other methods to engage youth. Scout's Own is included in the course to be a guide to scouts who serve as Chaplain's aide. Reference material for Scouts is the Patrol Leader's Handbook. On-line Registration. Contact Bill Wilson if you have questions. wrw@billwilsonlaw.com

Who: Youth desiring to be trained in the Leadership Skills required for their positions.
What: The purpose of the ILST course is to teach Scouts with leadership positions about their new roles and how to most effectively reach success in that role. ILST is the first course in the series of leadership training offered to Scouts and is a replacement for Troop Leadership Training. This next course in the series is National Youth Leadership Training.
When: April 30-May 2-2022. Arrive at 8:00 A .M. on April 30, 2022. Dismiss at 11:00 AM on May 1, 2022.
Where: Camp Wisdom
Staff Needed: Both youth and adult staff needed. Please register.
How: Registration Link for both participants and staff, now available.
Deadline: Registration closes April 24, 2022
