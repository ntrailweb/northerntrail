Plan your Summertime Activities Now

Summertime is a great time to schedule fun activities with your pack or troop. Examples are outings, pizza parties, movie nights and more. Use your imagination. These activities are a great way to keep the energy in your unit high and recruit new members. These activities don't have to be complicated, just follow youth protection guidelines and comply with the guide to safe scouting. Remember, SAFE:

  • Youth are Supervised by qualified and trustworthy adults who set the example for safety.
  • Activities are Assessed for risks.
  • Pre-requisite Fitness and skill levels are confirmed before participation.
  • Appropriate Equipment is utilized and Environmental conditions are monitored.

Training Details 2022

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Time to sign up for BALOO

BALOO is Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation. BSA requires 2 BALOO trained adults for each Cub Scout campout. This course is for all levels of Adult Camping experience. We teach the basics needed for you and your scouts to be comfortable in the Outdoors. Topics include, selecting gear, how to light a stove and cook camp meals, knot tying , water safety, basic first aid, fun games for campouts and how to plan a campfire. Come on down to Camp Wisdom April 30 for a fun, informative weekend. Black pug sign up link.

ALT / IOLS training for Scouts BSA

Scouting works when we have trained leaders and parents. Parents must understand the how and why of scouting. Adult Leader Training, ALT is an introduction to scouting and demonstrates in depth the AIMs and Methods of scouting. A scout’s chances of success in the program will be dramatically improved with trained and involved parents.

Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills, IOLS complements ALT training with details on the how and why of the outdoor program. Participants will function as scouts on an actual campout and be introduced to the details of a successful outdoor program.

Scoutmasters, Assistant Scoutmasters and Committee chairs must be fully trained, and this includes both ALT and IOLS. We encourage parents to, if possible, attend both ALT and IOLS but at a minimum ALT. ALT: Saturday April 9, 2022, at John D. Murchison Scouting Center (Dallas Scouting office). IOLS weekend of Apr 30, 2022, at Camp Wisdom. Sign-up here.

Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops

ILST (Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops) is scheduled for April 30-May 1, 2022, at Camp Wisdom. This is for any Scout with a leadership position on the Patrol Leader Council. The course teaches troop organization, leadership skills, how to teach using EDGE and is designed to teach by using games, reflections and other methods to engage youth. Scout's Own is included in the course to be a guide to scouts who serve as Chaplain's aide. Reference material for Scouts is the Patrol Leader's Handbook. On-line Registration. Contact Bill Wilson if you have questions. wrw@billwilsonlaw.com

Who: Youth desiring to be trained in the Leadership Skills required for their positions.
What: The purpose of the ILST course is to teach Scouts with leadership positions about their new roles and how to most effectively reach success in that role. ILST is the first course in the series of leadership training offered to Scouts and is a replacement for Troop Leadership Training. This next course in the series is National Youth Leadership Training.
