It’s Vitally Important, Lend a Hand!

I recently attended a session at the University of Scouting on leader burnout. There was one person in the room from another district who was clearly very distraught. She and her husband were trying to hold a Cub Scout Pack together and were obviously frustrated by the lack of help from the parents in the unit. While the exact reason they were not getting help was not clear, this is unfortunately not a terribly uncommon problem with scout units. Read more.

District Banquet

This coming Sunday January 26, Buy your tickets today. It's fast and easy to buy them online. Advanced cash or check sales available, contact Will Hall. william.hall@scouting.org

Why come? Four great reasons; 1) Show your appreciation to hard working volunteers who make the program possible, 2) Hear an inspiring keynote about scouting. 3) Enjoy a great meal in a wonderful facility, 4) Spend some time reconnecting with scout friends.

Opening ceremony begins at 5:45PM, doors open at 4:30PM. Brookhaven Country Club.

Scouting for Food

We've made great progress organizing Scouting for Food, but there are still many areas without coverage. Every door without a bag is a lost opportunity help feed the hungry in our community. Bags go out on Saturday Feb 8th and are picked up on Saturday February 15th. There is still time to get signed up.

You can arrange to pick up bags by contacting Greg Lewis, Scouting for Food, Chairman greg.lewis@att.net m: 972.523.1246
