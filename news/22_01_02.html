New Year

This is the time when many of use make resolutions for the new year. Resolutions are really about thinking about what we want to accomplish and then putting a plan into action to meet these goals. For scouting the goals might be to hold more unit campouts or other activities. In my experience these events usually turn out well and scouts and scouters are glad they participated. Even if conditions aren't perfect. On one very memorable weekend campout our troop woke up to snow covered tents. An unusual experience for scouts and adults. So make a resolution to have scouting adventures in 2022.

Re-Charter

Recharter all done? congratulations! Unfortunately we have a few units with delinquent charters. Without a charter you don't have a scout unit. Scouts can't advance, you can't hold meetings or participate in scouting events.

We have entered a period where an expired charter can still be renewed, but soon that will pass and then instead of a re-charter, you will have to start over and get a new charter. This is much more involved, so please don't wait any longer

Help is available, all you have to do is ask. Contact your unit commissioner or Steve Bloberger who can be reached at: steve_bloberger@icloud.com

Religious Emblems

Make earning religious emblems part of your program. Just about every faith tradition has a religious emblem. The religious emblems program is recognized by BSA as a way for Scouts in all age groups to develop a deeper understanding of their faith. To get started, speak with your spiritual advisor or visit https://www.scouting.org/awards/religious-awards/.
