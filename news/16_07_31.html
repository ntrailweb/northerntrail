Second Annual Outdoor Adventure

Mark your Calendar; Saturday September 10, 2016, Yale Park, Map

Help us kick off our Fall recruiting by participating in the 2nd Annual Outdoor Adventure community event! Northern Trail District and the Girl Scouts of Northeast Texas are sponsoring a family day at the park for people residing in the Richardson Independent School District. Last year we had over 25 activities and hundreds of families around the Richardson area enjoying a fun-filled day of games and scout skills.

This event is a wonderful opportunity to promote your pack, troop or crew and demonstrate how much fun it is to be a Scout! If you would like to sponsor and staff an activity, we will have a signup form on the website soon. If you have any questions, please email Marjie Price at msmn1204@gmail.com.

Fall Training, Mark your Calendar

Training dates have been posted to the calendar. If you have leaders who lack training, please insure that they allocate the time to attend training. Training is not just for front line leaders. Parents who want their scout to get the most out of the program, should attend training and learn just how the scouting program works. Training will make your job easier and you will get much better results.

Circle Ten Train-O-Ree, Saturday, October 1, Camp Wisdom Leadership Development Center. Leader-specific training for scoutmaster/ASM, cubmaster/ACM. Face-to-Face YPT. Contact: Trey Otte at trey@otte.us, 214.902.6776; registration: https://circle10.camp-master.com

Scoutmaster Leader Specific Training begins Wednesday October 12. This training is not just for Scoutmasters, but is designed for all adult leaders and parents in the Boy Scout program. This course is the first of a two part program. Topics covered include leadership styles, youth leadership development, patrol leaders' council, teaching Scouting skills, the Scoutmaster's conference, and growing the troop. More information and signup will be available soon.

Several training courses will be offered on a "Day of Training" Saturday October 15th. Details to come, but there will be training for Cubmaster, Den Leader, Committee member and more.

Backcountry Advanced Training will be offered beginning Thursday October 20th. Backcountry Advanced Training is an interactive course designed for those Individuals and Units who have been on or are preparing for a high-adventure trek and for those interested in organizing first time backcountry activities for their Unit. Two classroom sessions are followed by a weekend backpacking campout. The course features information and instruction detailing trek preparation, crew dynamics, and techniques of backpacking - hiking, camping, and preparing meals on the trail. More details soon.

Introduction to Outdoor Leadership Skills will be held the weekend of October 28th. This is the second part of the two part Scoutmaster training and again is designed for all adult leaders and parents. Participants will prepare for and go on an actual scout campout. Learn by doing and get all of your questions answered about how the outdoor programs works and why it is so attractive to the scouts. More information and signup will be available soon.

Online training is available on the BSA national website: my.scouting.org/ . If you don't have an account you will need to create one.

Popcorn

It’s time to sign up for the 2016 Popcorn World Series fundraiser. We are gearing up for our best year yet, and some exciting changes have been made to the program, including a new vendor (CAMP MASTERS), new popcorn selections and more. Popcorn kernel training is just around the corner, with sessions being held on Aug. 2, 4, 6, 9, 11 & 13. You will need to register for one of these “batting practices” within the next week. Before you can attend a training session, you must first register your pack/troop/team for popcorn sales. Visit the Council website to sign up, for additional details and for critical dates at circleten.org/popcorn.

In addition, you won’t want to miss out on an opportunity to attend the exciting and motivating Michael Beck Sales Training, to be held at AMC Valley View 16 on Thursday, August 18. Selling popcorn is easy with the right training and support, and achieving high popcorn sales goals will help fund both your unit operations and the Council’s programming. Selling popcorn also teaches Scouts the value of contributing their time and effort to making the Scouting program the best it can be. Register TODAY!
