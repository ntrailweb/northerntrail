Camp Wisdom Celebrates 100 Years

Camp Wisdom is celebrating 100 years of Scouting, and I wanted to learn more about the story behind this iconic camp. While there is a lot of information available, some of it is inaccurate.

The donation of the land came about due to an accidental encounter by John S. Wisdom. At the time, Wisdom was negative about the new Scouting movement, believing that boys should be doing something useful rather than running around wild. He had a reputation for being a bit grumpy.

One day, while out on his property, Wisdom heard voices on an adjacent plot of land. He investigated and found a troop of Scouts holding a service, giving thanks for the woods, the streams, and the birds. This encounter changed Wisdom's opinion of Scouting, and he invited the Scouts to use his land anytime they wished.

Eventually, Wisdom made the generous donation of his entire farm to the Scouts, and Camp Wisdom was born.

If you're interested in learning more about the history of Camp Wisdom and Scouting in the Circle Ten Council, I recommend the book Where Character is Caught by David C. Scott. Even 100 years ago, the value of Scouting was recognized, and this triggered amazing generosity of both time and treasure. Scouting is truly a gift!

It is Time to Recognize Adult Scouters

It is this time of year when we recognize our fellow scouters for the exemplary volunteer work that they do at the unit, district, and council levels. We remain thankful to them for their tireless service.

We have several awards to recognize these individuals beginning at the unit level with the Your Extraordinary Service Award or the Y.E.S. Award. At the district level we have two awards: the District Award of Merit and the Robert E. Little Award. Applications for these awards must be submitted no later than Friday, December 1st. No applications will be accepted after this date.

At the Council level we have the Silver Beaver Award. Applications for the Silver Beaver Award must be received no later than Monday, November 20th. No applications will be accepted after this date.

Self-nominations will not be accepted.

More information, including the submission details of these awards, is available here.

Recharter Time

The annual recharter process is a critical activity which must be completed for your unit to continue to operate. A charter is an agreement between your chartering organization, the Circle Ten Council and the National BSA organization. Every member of your unit both scout and adult must be properly accounted for with paid dues and any required training. Please do your part. Waiting until the last minute is hard on everyone.
