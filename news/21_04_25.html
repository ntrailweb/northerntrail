Responsibility

While not specifically one of the elements of the oath and law, it is woven into these principles and the scouting program. Showing up in the proper uniform and prepared to participate in a meeting is one example. Carefully planning and preparing for a campout with the proper equipment is another. Patrol leaders must take responsibility for their patrols and ensure that events are properly planned. Will scouts fall short of these things? Of course, but with practice and repeated experiences they will improve and accept responsibility. Responsible scouts become responsible adults and responsible adults are better life partners, employees, and citizens. Scouting is about building character and it is a process.

Congratulations, you have achieved the rank of Life Scout!

Now what? Well, of course, you need 6-months as an active Scout and 6-months of leadership to progress to the next rank (EAGLE!). You also must check off all the requirements of the Eagle Scout Application (Download it from this page: https://www.scouting.org/resources/guide-to-advancement/eagle-scout-rank/). While you are in that web site, READ that cover page from top to bottom. Always get your application and project workbook from links available on this page. That way you know you have the most current versions (a key factor) before you start getting signature approvals. Continue reading.

On-Line Pack/Troop Committee Member Training

Pack Committee member training requires only 53 minutes of your time and Troop Committee member training is 102 minutes. Both provide critical information about the role of the committee in unit operations. A well-run committee will help your unit run more effectively and efficiently. Check out this and other training opportunities at my.scouting.org.

National Youth Leadership Training

National Youth Leadership Training is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. Learn more.
