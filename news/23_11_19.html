Youth Training Opportunities

We’ve talked a lot about adult leader training, but scouting offers many opportunities for scouts to get leadership training. Leadership roles are required for Star, Life and Eagle rank advancement.

Leadership development is an integral component of life skills training. By assuming leadership roles, scouts gain valuable experiences that foster their growth as citizens, employees, and parents. These experiences equip them with the understanding and resilience to navigate the complexities of life's challenges.

Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops or ILST is a weekend leadership training program for Scouts BSA youth. There are age and rank requirements to be eligible. More information here.

National Youth Leadership Training or NYLT is an intense six-day course for youth members of troops, crews and ships. The content is delivered in a troop and patrol outdoor setting. It is designed to be fun, challenging and is a life changing experience. A scout must have completed ILST and meet other requirements to be eligible. More information here.

National Advanced Youth Leadership Experience or NALYE is available at both Philmont and the Bechtel Summit high adventure bases. More information here.

Recharter Time

Those of us who have been involved with scouting for many years know that the recharter process can be a little painful. Avoid the pain in two ways, first don't wait until the last minute. The second way to avoid headaches is to follow the procedure and ask questions if you have any doubts. Seeming minor things like signatures will land your recharter in the "defective" pile and the problems will need to be worked.

If you are not responsible for recharter in your unit, make sure your dues are paid and any required training is complete. An out-of-date YPT card is a non-starter! Please do your part. Waiting until the last minute is hard on everyone.

Youth Protection Training Current?

Your unit cannot complete Recharter if your YPT card is not current. Time flies, check your card and take the training if needed. Get training at: my.scouting.org. Don't wait, get done now.

Unit leaders, check the status of the adults in your unit now online.

Adult Scouter Recognition Awards Banquet

Our annual adult awards banquet will be held on February 22, 2024, 6:45p.m. First United Methodist Church, Richardson. Tickets may be purchased online here

Please help us recognize these hard working volunteers by nominating them for an award.

You can nominate someone in your unit for the Your Extraordinary Service award, which recognizes volunteers at the unit level. The District Award of Merit and the Robert E. Little Award recognize exceptional service by scouters at the district level. At the council level the Silver Beaver Award recognizes scouters who have provided exceptional long-term service to scouting.

Leaders work hard and sometimes worry that their efforts are not appreciated. An award is tangible evidence that yes, their contributions are valued. It does not take much time to nominate someone, however the impact can be substantial.

Please don't delay, get your nominations in as soon as possible.

For any questions, please contact the Awards and Recognition Chair, Muhammad Ikram at mzi@gatech.edu.

Winter Camp Trevor Rees-Jones Scout Camp December 27- 31, 2023

Circle Ten Council’s annual Winter Camp at Trevor Rees-Jones Scout Camp is a special jamboree-style camping event for Scouts BSA Troops, Venturing Crews, and Sea Scout Ships. It is the largest annual event put on by Circle Ten Council and is staffed by Circle Ten’s Mikanakawa Lodge of the Order of the Arrow.

Units camp jamboree style with multiple Units in a single campsite. The chance to make new friends from outside your immediate Scouting circle is one of the key rewards. Food is prepared by each Unit in their campsites (with the exception of the optional closing night BBQ). More information.

Membership + Recruiting
