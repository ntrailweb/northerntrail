Delinquent Charter?

If you are reading this and have not re-chartered your unit, you are now delinquent. All is not lost, while you are in the doghouse, there is a grace period before your unit is dropped. So please take steps to get this done ASAP. No excuses, if you need help, help is available. Contact Joe Tavares tavares.joseph@gmail.com or Chad Caudle chad.caudle@scouting.org. Your scouts are depending on you to complete this important task.

If your unit chair is hounding you about Youth Protection Training, application form or dues, please heed the request and complete these tasks ASAP. He or she can't complete the re-charter without your help. Online training is available here: my.scouting.org/. The training does not take very long and is not difficult.

Please, if you have not done so, send a copy of your unit roster to Chad Caudle chad.caudle@scouting.org ASAP. This will be a tremendous help to Chad.

Kidde Fire Extinguisher Recall

Nearly 38 million fire extinguishers manufactured by Kidde and sold between 1973 and 2017 are part of a nationwide recall. Scout leaders should check their unit meeting sites, troop trailers, trunks and garages today to see whether their fire extinguishers are included. There have been nearly 400 reports of affected models failing to activate in a fire emergency, according to the Consumer Product Safety Commission. The CPSC cites one death and 16 injuries. The affected models of Kidde (pronounced “kidda”) include plastic-handle fire extinguishers and plastic push-button fire extinguishers. Check kidde.com for list of specific units which are being recalled.
