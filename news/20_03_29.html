Adult Leadership Training (Leader Specific Training)

Course Canceled

Northern Trail ALT (Adult Leadership Training) /IOLS (Introduction to Outdoor Leadership Skills) training is a destination. Over the past 5 months we have improved the course to make it more interactive and a more fun learning atmosphere. It is with a heavy heart that we have no choice but to CANCEL the Spring 2020 ALT/IOLS course. As you may be aware, the City of Richardson has declared a Shelter-In-Place order until April 30th.

We already have a fall ALT/IOLS course scheduled and look forward to helping you be trained either for your position or as an adult volunteer in your troop. Scout leaders should be trained. As we hope to have summer camp and many other scouting opportunities between now and our next ALT/IOLS session we encourage you to look for online training for “Leadership Specific” & “Introduction to Outdoor Leadership Skills.” We appreciate your service and hope to see you in our fall training classes. If you have already registered for the Spring Courses the course director will be in contact with you. Please stay well & pray for our world health.

YIS Bram Franklin- ALT/IOLS N/T Course Director.

Philmont Wood Badge Course August 17 - 22, 2020

Get signed up today to attend this course at Philmont this summer!
Wood Badge is Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training for adults; designed for leaders of all BSA programs to help show those leaders how to ensure their youth get the most out of the Scouting program. Wood Badge consists of two parts. The first is a five-day practical program, usually offered in two 3-day camping experiences. The second part, completed over the following 18 months, requires participants to apply the skills they have learned in their local Scouting program by completing five goals (tickets) leading towards the participant's vision.

District Commissioner Minute

This is a difficult time for all of us. We’ve had plans cancelled, had to cancel events and had to hold virtual meetings. I hope that your dens and packs, patrols and troops, and crews have been able to adapt to using new methods to continue your youths Scouting journey. Just to remind you, your commissioner corps consists of volunteer Scouter who works directly with Scout units and leaders to help them provide the highest quality Scouting program possible. As friends, teachers and counselors to unit leaders, Commissioners represent the ideals, principles and policies of the Boy Scout program while providing the resources of the district and council to the units they serve. The primary purpose of the Unit Commissioner is to help units succeed.

Do you need help creating a recruiting plan, your commissioner can help. Do you need to find a new place to go camping, your commissioner can help. Are your ceremonies tried, your commissioner can help. Need to connect with other Scouters, your commissioner and attending Roundtable can help. The possibilities are endless, but communication is a two-way street-your commissioners corps can’t help you unless you let them. If you don’t know who your unit commissioner is reach out to me at hjv3@outlook.com or 214-769-5040. If you haven’t heard from your commissioner in a while, contact them.

Yours in Scouting,
Hank Voegtle, District Commissioner
