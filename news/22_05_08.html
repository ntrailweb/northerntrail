Twilight Camp 2022

Where can Cub Scouts have fun and work on advancement at the same time? The answer is simple—at the Wild, Wild West Twilight Camp. Northern Trail will be having Twilight Camp at Richardson’s Breckenridge Park from June 6-10.

Twilight Day Camp is for Cub and Webelos Scouts and is a late afternoon, early evening activity making it available to working parents. Activities start at 4:30pm and runs through 8:30pm each day. Lots of activities including crafts, BB-guns, archery, games, crafts and scout skills. We will have the ever-popular egg drop contest. Registration for both scouts and adult leaders is now available. https://scoutingevent.com/571-2022NTDayCamp

If you have a Cub Scout attending Twilight Camp, we need your participation too. The easiest way is to serve as a Walking Den Leader. A Walking Den Leader is just that, a wrangler who rides herd over 8 Cubs, moseying them over from activity to activity. You’ll cast your gander on a wide range of Cub Scout activities and get a first look at a craft project that might just become a family treasure.

There are Camp staff positions as well both adult and junior. Sign up using the link above.

Don't forget, every adult in camp must have a current YPT card!

Richardson Community Band Summer Concert Series

The Richardson Community Band needs scout volunteers to assist with their summertime outdoor performances.

Scouting is about service, and this is a great opportunity for your unit to demonstrate community service.

Tasks include unloading equipment and setting up chairs at the Civic Center. A the end of the performance scouts assist with returning the chairs to storage and loading the equipment trailer.

At the beginning of the performance the Scouts present the colors and lead the audience in the Pledge of Allegiance and National Anthem.

Dates:
Sundays - 7:00 pm – Richardson Civic Center Lawn (we meet around 6:00 pm for set up - earlier in May to learn the ropes)
May 29 (Memorial Day and opening of our Summer Series)
June 12, June 26, July 24, August 7, August 21

Scouts Deserve Trained Leaders

Knowing how to do something makes a job much easier. Many of us may think we know all there is to know about scouting, but in truth we can all benefit from training. With training you will spend less time dealing with problems and more time delivering the program.

Training prepares us to accomplish goals quickly, safely and with high quality results.

We have training for parents, front line leaders and scouts. In person training starts soon, get signed up today. All adults must have current youth protection training. There are many on-line courses available at my.scouting.org. Check it out today! More details below.

Upcoming Training opportunities

May 20-22, 2022 Wilderness First Aid, Richardson
Oct 21, 2022IOLS, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills
Oct 21, 2022BALOO, Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

FBI Dallas 2022 Teen Academy

On Thursday, July 14, 2022, 8am to 5pm, the FBI Dallas Division will once again host the FBI Dallas Teen Academy.

The Teen Academy is a one day overview of the FBI for high school seniors and students that just graduated high school. The day will begin at 8:00 am and conclude by 5:00 pm.

Due to the ongoing pandemic and continued changes in protocols, guidelines and requirements will be provided when selection notifications are provided. Please note, if the policy doesn’t change, you must be fully vaccinated or show a negative dated (within 3 days of event) COVID test result. The maximum number of participants will be 30 unless some restrictions are lifted.

Application, forms and essay must be completed and sent to Angela Harris via email or mail, no later than May 13, 2022 . If the applicant is selected to participate, more information will be provided.

The application can be found here: https://www.fbi.gov/file-repository/dallas-teen-academy-2022-021122.pdf/view. Applicants will need to scroll to the bottom and download the application and forms. Please direct any questions to Angela Harris at: CommOutreach.Dallas@fbi.gov.
