Momentum

All of us have been impacted by this virus, however life has gone on and we must do our best to continue to make our community better. Part of that process is training a new generation of leaders to meet the challenges yet to come. Historically, scouting has made huge contributions to the success of our communities. Scouting values and skills are vital tools. As adult leaders in our units, we must strive to keep the momentum by holding meetings, campouts, events and getting trained. This can be done safely, with the proper preparation. Regular meeting place not available? Find another time and place to meet. Afraid to go camping? Learn about new protocols and how they have been successfully and safely implemented. Need advice? Talk to your unit commissioner, attend Roundtable, talk to scouter's in other units, check out the council website and other resources. Don't delay, while things are improving, we can't wait, build back the momentum today!

Spring NYLT

National Youth Leadership Training in 2021. First course will be during spring break: March 14-19, 2021. NYLT is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. More information.

ILST

April 10-11, 2021 at Camp Wisdom. The purpose of the ILST (Introduction to Leadership Skills for Troops, (formally Oak Leaf) course is to teach Scouts with leadership positions about their new roles and how to most effectively reach success in that role. It is intended to help Scouts in leadership positions within their troop understand their responsibilities and to equip them with organizational and leadership skills to fulfill those responsibilities. Circle Ten COVID-19 Guidelines

We have always had and continue to be concerned about safety. Risk cannot be eliminated entirely; however, we can work to minimize the risks. COVID-19 has added another dimension. Just like other safety rules, follow the procedures and keep everyone safe. Don't let the virus win, scout on!

Keep Calm and CAMP On

The weather is great, and the camps are open, it's time to get out there and enjoy them. The COVID safety protocols have been proven and are in place to ensure a safe activity. Sign-up for a camp site and view the guidelines! ScoutsBSA, Venturing and Sea scouts may make reservations at council camps. Cub Scout units may only camp as part of a district or council activity. If winter camping, be sure and bring water should the camp supply be offline to due freezing weather.
