      Roundtable meeting

      Roundtable is starting back up for the fall. Please come and join in. The meetings are 2nd Thursday of every month at 7:00 pm at First United Methodist Church Richardson, Rm 230

      Roundtables are monthly meetings for Scouting leaders, held at the district level, and designed to provide supplemental training, program ideas, and fellowship. These meetings are a key component of unit support, offering opportunities for leaders to share experiences, receive information on policies and events, and connect with other leaders in their area.

      Training

      S.A.F.E. guarding Youth

      The new course contains a lot of updated information and reinforces a culture of active supervision, situational awareness, and safety. S.A.F.E. guarding Youth training is 90 minutes long the first time you complete it. After completing the training one time, you will only need to complete annual 15-minute refreshers moving forward.

      üêü Certified Angler Instructor (CAI) Course on Zoom. Do you have a passion for fishing that you have been wanting to share with others but just didn't know what you needed to do? Your chance is here! Strengthen your Council‚Äôs Fishing Program by taking the Certified Angler Instructor (CAI) Course! CAI is a 14-hour training course(2 hours on Zoom for 4 weeks) for Scouters and Scouts aged 15 and above. As a CAI, you will learn to plan and run successful youth fishing education and events for Cub Scouts, Scouts BSA, and Venturing crews. Graduates will be expected to lead fishing events and education in their council, and see the smiles that result when our youth catch fish.

      2025 Wood Badge 147

      Wood Badge brings together leaders from all areas of Scouting - Cub Scouting, Scouts BSA, Sea Scouts, and Venturing. Our mission is to inspire and train adults through world class leadership training that sets an example for youth empowerment. Go to Black Pug Circle Ten Council offers three Wood Badge courses in 2025 to meet the varying schedules and needs of our volunteers. In 2025, there is a spring, summer, and fall course so you may choose the time of year that works best for you.
