Training Checkup

Scouts deserve trained leaders and some training is required. Every registered adult must have current Youth Protection Training. This training is good for two years and may be obtained online. In addition front line leaders must be position trained. Don't delay get trained. You can check your training record online and obtain training online in addition to live training opportunities within Northern Trail. Go to my.scouting.org and log into to your account, if you don't have an account then set one up. Questions to Lester Cox: lester.cox@tx.rr.com

BSA Adult Leader Training / Intro to Outdoor Leader Skills

This training is for BSA scout leaders and parents of scouts who want a deeper understanding of the program and a taste of leadership without making a commitment. This is a two-part course, the first part in in a classroom setting and the second part is in the field at a hands-on camp-out. Classroom sessions will be held September 11, 2019 and September 18, 2019, 7-9:30 PM. The field training camp-out will be held Saturday, September 28 & 29, 2019, 8AM Saturday to noon Sunday at Camp Wisdom. The fee is $40 payable at the first classroom session. Advanced registration required. Register here.

Backcountry Advanced Training

BAT is an interactive course designed for those Individuals and Units who are preparing for a high-adventure trek and for those interested in organizing first time backcountry activities for their Unit. The course is titled “Leave the Trailer Behind!” and is directed towards Individuals and Units that want to introduce more backpacking to their annual program. This is a great opportunity as a Unit activity for new or seasoned backpackers or to prepare for future backpacking treks. Two classroom sessions are followed by a weekend backpacking camp-out adventure. The course features information and instruction detailing trek preparation, crew dynamics, and techniques of backpacking - hiking, camping, and preparing meals on the trail. Read more and register.
