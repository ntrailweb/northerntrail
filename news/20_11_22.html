Winter camp December 27-31

Circle Ten Council’s annual Winter Camp at Trevor Rees-Jones Scout Camp is a special jamboree-style camping event for Scouts BSA troops, Venturing Crews and Sea Scout Ships. It is the largest annual event put on by Circle Ten Council and is staffed by Circle Ten’s Mikanakawa Lodge of the Order of the Arrow. Units camp jamboree style with multiple units in a single campsite. The chance to make new friends from outside your immediate Scouting circle is one of the key rewards. Food is prepared by each unit in their campsites (with the exception of the optional closing night BBQ).

Each morning, Scouts can work on merit badges, take part in special living history programs in the Mountain Man, Indian Village and Lumberjack areas or learn new program skills. Afternoons offer a wide-range of activities including competitive games, shooting sports, climbing, activities in the living history areas, horseback riding, wood-turning and more! Evenings include special events like a Native American Pow Wow, Cowboy Action Shoot competition, Star Watch parties or just relaxing in the campsite around the campfire.

Membership is a Year Round Activity

Scouting only works with a continuous progression, scouts join they advance, move on and new scouts join. Without new members, new potential scouts won't benefit from the program. Allowing your unit to dwindle and die, is not fair to those who came before you who worked so hard to create the program you have enjoyed. Take the time to recruit and keep your unit strong and healthy ready to serve the next generation of scouts.

Our Northern Trail membership committee is working hard to help you achieve these goals. Please participate in these efforts.

Every Scouting family should have a yard sign, either Cub Specific or generic. Yard signs will demonstrate that Scouting is thriving in Northern Trail.

If you have any questions, please call Bram Franklin, Northern Trail Membership Committee Chair. bramfranklin@gmail.com (214) 609.9089.
