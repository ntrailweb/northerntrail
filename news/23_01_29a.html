Community Service and Passive Recruiting

Recently I read an article in Scouting Magazine about passive recruiting. Another good reason for your unit to participate in community service projects. Years ago when my boys were in scouts, our unit would sing holiday carols each year at a nursing home. The scouts would bring their band instruments and they would do their best to sing. We'd bring cookies and some punch. The scouts would visit with the residents and we always had a good time. One year for whatever reason we did not go and the following year we were told that they were so looking forward to our annual visit and were quite disappointed when we did not come. Clearly we made a lot of very positive impressions in spite of our mediocre musical ability😉

As the article points out events like these often lead to new scouts for your unit. Sometimes in very indirect paths.

Community service is a part of being a scout, it's part of the oath and law. In addition to teaching your scouts what it means to be a scout, it can also lead to new scouts for your unit. Part of community service is making the gift of scouting available to youth in your community. The first step is awareness of the program and why it's a good thing.

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.
