Training for Adults

Scouting is a unique concept, and the aims and methods are not immediately obvious. It can be a little overwhelming to someone new. This problem has been addressed and there is a wealth of training available to help ensure the success or your child and unit.

Roundtable Topic

The number one cause of death behind car accidents of people under the age of 25 is suicide. With the lockdowns and lack of social interaction cause by covid, the suicide prevention hotlines in parts of the country saw an 8,000% increase in traffic.

Our next roundtable will play host to mental health professionals to discuss this and other mental health issues relevant to scouting, Join us at 7:30 on Thursday April, 8 on Zoom for this discussion and to learn about how we can provide hope to those who need it most.

Training for Scouts

National Youth Leadership Training

National Youth Leadership Training is an exciting, action-packed program designed for councils to provide youth members with leadership skills and experience they can use in their home troops and in other situations demanding leadership of self and others.

The NYLT course centers around the concepts of what a leader must BE, what he must KNOW, and what he must DO. The key elements are then taught with a clear focus on HOW TO. The skills come alive during the week as the patrol goes on a Quest for the Meaning of Leadership. Learn more.

Other Opportunities

50th Anniversary Environmental Protection Agency Award

The Environmental Protection Agency, as part of a public awareness campaign to celebrate its first 50 years, has partnered with the Boy Scouts of America to create an award for members of troops, crews and ships. This new award will recognize youth who research and learn about the subjects of human health and the environment, as the EPA develops its vision for the next 50 years. More information and an application.

Religious Emblem Program

The religious emblems programs are programs created by the various religious groups to encourage youth to grow stronger in their faith. The religious groups - not Boy Scouts of America - have created the religious emblems programs themselves. Contact Hank Voegtle hankusv@gmail.com if you have questions.

Fund Raising

Like it or not, any organization requires funds to make it operate. Scouting is a very efficient operation with volunteer leaders who do the heavy lifting. We have camps, offices and professional scouters who provide essential services. These professionals represent a small fraction of the total. Funds are raised in several ways including fees, popcorn sales, camp cards and direct contributions from individuals and groups. All these income sources have declined due to COVID has this has placed a severe strain on the organization and it is vital that we make an extra effort to get back on track.

COVID19 Mask Update

As you may be aware, Governor Greg Abbott lifted state COVID directives, encouraging all Texans to take personal responsibility for their own safety and the safety of others.

Circle Ten Council is committed to the safety of its members, so at this time we have not made any changes to our current COVID-19 safety protocols.

Proven methods are in place to have activities safely. Do not stay home, make scouting happen, just continue to stay vigilant to ensure the safety of all participants. If updates are made to our safety procedures, we will share them broadly.
