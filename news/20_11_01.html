Re-charter, Time to Get Cracking

Every unit should have logged in by now. We are still seeing units which have not started the process. Waiting until the last minute is a recipe for pain, not only for unit leaders, but unit commissioners, our district executive and the hard working folks at the council office. Help avoid the pain, get started today.

A big stumbling block will be confirming that every adult on your roster has a current Youth Protection Training. Youth Protection Training is good for two years. This is a showstopper so you must get this done as quickly as possible.

There is a re-charter clinic on Wednesday November 4th at 6:00PM on Zoom. Bring your questions.

Roundtable Trunk or Treat?

No, not a late Halloween event, our November Roundtable will feature our “Cooking with Northern Trail Thanksgiving Special.” We will be setting up in person and online on Thursday, November 12 in the west parking lot (across Custer Road) of First United Methodist Church Richardson for a practical demonstration of making a traditional Thanksgiving dinner using Dutch oven cookery. This Roundtable special event is for all adult leaders-Cub Scouts, Scouts BSA, Venturing and Sea Scouts.

So, join us on November 12 at 7:30 P.M. and learn some Dutch oven cooking tricks, enjoy fellowship with other Scouters and maybe get some samples.

Membership is a Year Round Activity

We, as Scouts BSA, need boys and girls as the recipients of teaching, coaching & mentoring our shared goals of character, ethics, leadership & fun. We need your help increasing our membership base. Our Northern Trail membership committee will be reaching out to every pack, troop, crew, ship & post to speak to your committee membership lead. If you do not have that volunteer in place on your team, please create one, it just takes asking. We will be asking them to be part of the Northern Trail membership team to help with recruitment, marketing ideas, and general N/T improvements to each team and our overall membership.

Every Scouting family should have a yard sign, either Cub Specific or generic. Yard signs will demonstrate that Scouting is thriving in Northern Trail.

If you have any questions, please call Bram Franklin, Northern Trail Membership Committee Chair. bramfranklin@gmail.com (214) 609.9089.

Recognize Adult Scouters

Scouting only exists due the tireless work of volunteers and the unit, district, council and national level. We are indebted to these individuals who are critical to the success of the scouting movement.

We have a number of awards to recognize these individuals beginning at the unit level with the Your Extraordinary Service Award or Y.E.S. At the district level we have two awards the District Award of Merit and the Robert E. Little Award. Applications for these awards must be submitted to Dr. Harry Yankuner hy18@sbcglobal.net no later than December 12th. Applications will not be accepted after this date, no exceptions.

At the Council level we have the Silver Beaver Award. Applications for the Silver Beaver Award must be received by Dr. Harry Yankuner hy18@sbcglobal.net no later than November 19th. Applications will not be accepted after this date, no exceptions.

Self nominations will not be accepted.
