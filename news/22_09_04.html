Training

Now is the time to get signed up for training. Unit leaders, send new parents to training.

Youth Protection Training (YPT) is only valid for two years. Every adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete this required task. On-line at my.scouting.org.

In addition to YPT, every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting. Leader Specific Training (LST) is coming up Saturday October 8th and is for Scouts BSA leaders and parents. Sign up on-line. This is a two part course and the outdoor part, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills (ILST) is the weekend of October 21-23. There are separate sign-ups for both. Basic Adult Outdoor Orientation (BALOO) for Cub Scout Leaders is the weekend of October 21-23.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required. On-line at my.scouting.org

More advanced training for high adventure treks like Philmont is available. Backcountry Advanced Training (BAT) begins October 12.

Webelos Woods, November 4-6, 2022

Webelos Woods is an awesome opportunity for Webelos (and their parents) to experience some of the fun that they can look forward to once they cross over into a troop. Troops will host Webelos Scouts from various packs and will form patrols which will include those Webelos Scouts for the weekend. Packs will be assigned to camp with a host Troop by the event staff.

On Saturday, the patrols will rotate in a round-robin style of activities where they can visit with different Troops. Each patrol will learn a variety of different outdoor and Scout skills and put them to use in live demonstrations. On Saturday night, both districts will come together to enjoy a rousing campfire program with yells and skits! There will also be a OA Brotherhood ceremony on Saturday evening.

Register today, early bird discount until 10/9/22. Limited space.

Annual Re-Charter

Every unit must re-charter their unit each year. It will be here before you know it!

Start now and this process will be much easier. Wait until the last minute and it is hard on everyone. If you are not in charge of re-charter, take the time to do your part by completing any required training, submitting applications and paying your dues.

Disaster Preparedness Program

Fretz Park Library Thursday Sept 8th 5:30 - 7:00 PM.

Are you prepared in the event there is an emergency? A tornado? An ice storm? Being prepared is vital for staying safe and weathering any emergency. Learn the basic elements of preparedness though activities and facilitated discussion with the City of Dallas' Office of Emergency Management. They will teach you to recognize the hazards, prepare you by sharing what you should have in an emergency kit and helping you to come up with a plan to keep you and your loved ones safe.

Presented by the City of Dallas' Office of Emergency Management.
