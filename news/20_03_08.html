Stay Healthy!

With this being Flu Season (still) and the concerns over Covid-19 (Corona Virus) please follow recommendations by your county, state and federal health departments. A few of those recommendations are listed below:

  • Have an emergency plan which should include a 2 - 4 week supply of prescription medications
  • Know and coordinate with your neighbors especially older neighbors
  • Stay at home if you are sick.
  • Wash hands often and efficiently, hand sanitizers with at least 60% alcohol when soap and water not available.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose and face.
  • Cough or sneeze into your handkerchief or elbow.
  • Avoid contact with people who are sick.
  • Clean and disinfect often frequently touched surfaces.
  • No handshaking! Use fist or elbow bump.
  • Use your knuckle to touch light switches, elevator buttons, etc.
  • Be aware of school dismissals
  • Supervise and remind your scouts and scouters to BE PREPARED and plan.
  • For more information go to www.ready.gov

Have Cubs Crossing Over to Scouts BSA?

Crossover season is underway and there are key things that packs and troops should know:
For Packs-all youth Cub Scout awards need to be recorded and uploaded through Scoutbook or internet advancement BEFORE youth are registered in a troop. Once a youth is registered in a troop, Cub Scout advancement will not be able to be recorded at the unit level (without a lot of trouble).

For Packs and Troops-if a Cub Scout was registered in a Pack at the time he or she crosses over, use the BSA transfer form, filestore.scouting.org/filestore/pdf/524-401WB.pdf, this will keep their records intact and avoid having to pay the $60 youth registration fee again.

Philmont Wood Badge Course August 17 - 22, 2020

Get signed up today to attend this course at Philmont this summer!
Wood Badge is Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training for adults; designed for leaders of all BSA programs to help show those leaders how to ensure their youth get the most out of the Scouting program. Wood Badge consists of two parts. The first is a five-day practical program, usually offered in two 3-day camping experiences. The second part, completed over the following 18 months, requires participants to apply the skills they have learned in their local Scouting program by completing five goals (tickets) leading towards the participant's vision.
