Friendly

The fourth point of the Scout Law is A Scout is Friendly. A Scout is a friend to all. He is a brother to other Scouts. He offers his friendship to people of all races and nations, and respects them even if their beliefs and customs are different from his own. Scouting reinforces this basic value.

Venturing Rendezvous Weekend 4/10-4/12, Camp Wisdom

Want to get away? How about a weekend getting to know other Venturers in Circle Ten?

Rendezvous is always full of activities and this one is no different: Shooting sports, Black powder shooting, Archery, Tomahawk throwing, Kayaking and canoeing, Fly fishing, Culinary class, STEM (Ham radio, Electricity, Welding, and Wood Turning), Low COPE, Climbing Wall, Distress signals with live flares, Driving Bobcats, Service Project, Training (youth & adults) and Saturday night PARTY with an up and coming LIVE ROCK BAND - Awaiting Daylight. Come by for the weekend or drop in Saturday morning and stay for the day (and the evening program).

Who Can Attend: This event is for anybody, ages 14-20 or those that are 13 and are currently in the eighth grade, that want to have some fun! Any participant who is not currently a member of a Scouting unit, must register for the event through a host Scouting unit. The appropriate number of male and female adult Advisors must accompany youth participants. If you have questions, or are interested in participating and need to contact a unit to register, please email VOA@circletenventuring.org. More information

Youth Protection Training is Required

Face to Face Youth protection is available at the Fairview office during April. More Face to Face YPT in May at First United Methodist Church. All leaders are required to have Youth Protection Training. The on-line version is available at myscouting.org, but the Face to Face version is required for Adult leaders at Twilight and Overnight Camps.
