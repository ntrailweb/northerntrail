Scouting For Food

February is "Do a good turn month".

Northern Trail District invites your unit to be part of the most visible activity of BSA, Scouting for Food.

Food insecurity continues to be a prevalent issue in our community, especially for children. 20% of children under the age of 18 are food insecure in Dallas County. "Food insecurity is defined by the USDA as the lack of access, at times, to enough food for an active, healthy life. Key drivers of food insecurity include unemployment, poverty, and income shocks, which can prevent adequate access to food." We can support The Network of Community Ministries in their mission to help the hungry in our area. One of the aims of Scouting is Character. Showing care for your fellow neighbor is a great way to display your Citizenship in the Community. Collecting food can help reduce food insecurity and give your scout a sense of helping to make someone’s life better.

This year, we are asking units to conduct a neighborhood food drive. Donations to be collected and delivered to the Network of Community Ministries on Saturday, February10 between the hours of 9a-2p.

Their address is 1500 International Parkway , Suite 300
Richardson, TX

Sign up is on Black Pug scoutingevent.com/571-2024NTSFF for more information contact Scouting for Food Chair: Dory Petsrillo, dlmp616@gmail.com or DE Katherine McCrary, Katherine.McCrary@scouting.org

Have your scouts aged out of scouting? Are you looking to apply your scouting skills? Membership + Recruiting

  • Follow up on New Members: Pack Unit Leaders, please make note of newly recruited members who you have not seen recently. A simple phone call, email or even a postcard can make the difference. Remember, you understand the value of the scout program, with new parents you must sell the program.
  • Grow your Membership: Growing your membership is a year-round activity. You should always be looking for opportunities to recruit more scouts into the program! If you need assistance with recruiting new members contact Katherine McCrary at katherine.mccrary@scouting.org for suggestions.
