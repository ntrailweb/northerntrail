Time Management

There is an old story about a demonstration which goes something like this. A teacher brings out a large jar in front of a group of students and begins carefully placing rocks in the jar. Eventually the jar will hold no more rocks and the teacher asks, "Is the jar full?" One of the students quickly says yes. The teacher brings out some pebbles and begins to add them between the bigger rocks, eventually no more pebbles can be added. "Is the jar full now?" Not immediately seeing where this is going, one student says yes. The teacher then brings out a jar of sand and with some effort is able to add sand such that no voids are apparent. Is it full now? The students are not so sure. The teacher brings out a large pitcher of water and pours the entire contents into the jar and the water rises to the rim. "Is it full now?" With some hesitation a few say yes.

There are several lessons in this story, first of course is that a problem may be more nuanced than it first appears. One lesson I have heard attached to this story is that with life, one needs to address the big things first. Make sure the important things get done first. Then you can find spaces for those other things you want to do. Another lesson is that while one may feel overwhelmed, there is more energy, time and opportunity than may be first apparent. Lastly my favorite life lesson is to remind scouts and adults that they can accomplish, learn and do more, much more than they think they can. Never underestimate your own potential. Coaxing that last bit of water into the jar may be difficult, but there is more room than you think!

Webelos Woods, November 4-6, 2022

Webelos Woods is an awesome opportunity for Webelos (and their parents) to experience some of the fun that they can look forward to once they cross over into a troop. Troops will host Webelos Scouts from various packs and will form patrols which will include those Webelos Scouts for the weekend. Packs will be assigned to camp with a host Troop by the event staff.

On Saturday, the patrols will rotate in a round-robin style of activities where they can visit with different Troops. Each patrol will learn a variety of different outdoor and Scout skills and put them to use in live demonstrations. On Saturday night, both districts will come together to enjoy a rousing campfire program with yells and skits! There will also be a OA Brotherhood ceremony on Saturday evening.

Training

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation (BALOO)
Saturday and Sunday October 22-23, 2022

Who: It is the Cub Scout leader training required for any Cub Scout den or pack outdoor event, including pack camping overnighters and Webelos den overnighters.

What: Leaders who complete BALOO training are better prepared to plan pack den or pack overnight events.You'll learn how to make camping activities and outings fun. You'll discover how to keep your Cub Scouts safe and entertained. And you'll learn how to plan an event that's rooted firmly in Cub Scouting's principles. At least one BALOO Trained leader is required for a Cub Scout group to conduct an overnight activity.

When: October 22-October 23, 2022. Participants should arrive at 8:00 a.m. on October 22st, 2022 and will be dismissed at 11:00 a.m. on October 23rd, 2022.

Annual Re-Charter

Every unit must re-charter their unit each year. This involves several steps. You can start working on this now.

  • Update rosters both youth and adult.
  • Review training status for every adult. Current Youth Protection is required.
  • Collect adult and scout applications if required.
  • Collect fees

Start now and this process will be much easier. Wait until the last minute and it is hard on everyone.
