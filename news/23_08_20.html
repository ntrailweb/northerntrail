It's Hot Out There

There is no doubt that this has been an unusually hot July and August and we have all been trying to beat the heat. Hydration is critical. For some reason, it can be difficult to consume enough fluid to replace what is lost. It’s critical to be proactive about your water intake in very hot weather. Make sure everyone in your party has adequate water and is consuming enough to stay hydrated. Don’t assume! Teach your scouts to be aware of the color of their urine. Copious and clear is the key to staying properly hydrated.

Heat exhaustion occurs when your body is no longer able to cool itself via perspiration. Like your car, if the warning light comes on, the time to act is now. Waiting will lead to much more serious problems. The next step is heat stroke. Victims of heat stroke will have elevated body temperature and will often start to stammer and be delirious. It can be very difficult to help someone in such a state. Get the person to cool place as soon as possible and get medical attention.

Upcoming Training

  • Youth Protection Training: This training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete this very necessary task. my.scouting.org
  • Additional Training Beyond position training: Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well. This and many other excellent training courses are available at my.scouting.org
  • In-person training: November 11th & 12th Details.
    • BALOO: – Every cub scout pack overnight camping event must have at least 1 trained BALOO certified adult leader. Please join us at Camp Wisdom for a fun filled weekend of BALOO training on November 11th & 12th.
    • Adult Leadership Training: (also known as Leadership Specific Training) – This is for all adults not just unit leaders. This course teaches you how a scout troop is run, from scout run leadership, recruiting, aims and methods, committee and how you can be a small or big part of your troop.
    • IOLS – Introduction to Leadership Skills: This course is the final step to wearing your trained patch. Whether you wear a uniform or not, this course teaches you the skills you will want to learn. Find out how fun adult leadership can be. Realize your issues and concerns are the same as other leaders. This is a day & a half course with one overnight camping, a great campfire program under the stars.

Wood Badge 142

Wood Badge is the Boy Scouts of America's ultimate leadership training designed to meet the advanced leadership needs of Scouters.

Wood Badge brings together leaders from all areas of Scouting - Cub Scouting, Scouts BSA and Venturing. Our mission is to inspire and train adults through world class leadership training that sets an example for youth empowerment.

Wood Badge 142 will be held at Camp Constantin the weekends of October 20-22, 2023 and November 4-5, 2023. Registration deadline October 10, 2023.
