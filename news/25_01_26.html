But, We’ve Always Done it That Way!

Change for the sake of change is rarely a good thing. We must adapt, and scouting has adapted with changing times and new realities. An example is expanding the program to include girls in all programs. Changing the name of the organization to better reflect the organization as a family-centered program better reflects the character of the movement.

There are aspects of scouting which are timeless, and these are embodied in the scout oath, law, methods and aims. The outdoor program focused on camping hiking and high adventure is still key to the success of scouting. This is accomplished by getting youth out of their everyday routines and providing them the opportunity for fun, new experiences, leadership and learning by experiencing the consequences of their decisions.

The safety of our scouts is of paramount importance while allowing scouts to fully experience the challenges. The information and the processes to keep our scouts safe, continues to evolve and the resources are available to every leader to ensure the safest possible environment.

Scouting works because it is a well thought out program which has stood the test of time. Our job as leaders is to deliver this program to the youth in our care and not reinvent the program to match our personal vision. There are plenty of opportunities for innovations like unique camping destinations and activities without attempting to change things like advancement processes and procedures.

2025 Backcountry Advanced Training (BAT)
Classroom Sessions, February 5 and 12, 2025
Practical February 21-23

BAT is an interactive course designed for those individuals and Units who are preparing for a high-adventure trek and for those interested in organizing first time backcountry activities for their Unit.

The course is titled "Leave the Trailer Behind!" and is directed towards individuals and Units that want to introduce more backpacking to their annual program. This is a great opportunity as a Unit activity for new or seasoned backpackers, or to prepare for future backpacking treks.

Two classroom sessions are followed by a weekend backpacking campout adventure. The course features information and instruction detailing trek preparation, crew dynamics, and techniques of backpacking - hiking, camping, and preparing meals on the trail. More information and registration.

Hazardous Weather Training Now Required

BSA Nationals updated reporting on Hazardous Weather Training requirements last week, resulting in an immediate and dramatic decline in the number of fully-trained direct contact leaders we have in this district.

If you have not taken hazardous weather in the past year, please find a few minutes to confirm the expiration dates and renew your training. The online course is only 28 minutes. The training is available by logging onto my.scouting.org and then use this link:https://training.scouting.org/courses/SCO_800

Youth Protection Training (YPT)

Avoid the rush, check your card and get it done. Training is valid for two years and you must retake the online course. If you are out of date, it is full stop. Go to my.scouting.org for this and other training opportunities.

Range Activities Safety Training

The Range Activities SAFEty training is now available online. Every unit who participates in Range Activities must have trained leaders at the range with their units. So this is not just directed at the Unit Leader but at whomever is accompanying their unit for a range day. If you are a direct contact leader, take the course. It requires about 30 minutes of your time. Once you login to my.scouting.org you can then follow this link directly to the course at https://training.scouting.org/courses/SCO_822
