Keeping the Fire Going

Recently I was watching a YouTube video and in the video a person had just started a cooking fire when a friend approached. He said to the friend; ‚Äúthis is a young fire and I need to attend to it.‚Äù Those of us who have built fires would know exactly what he was trying to say. Getting a fire going takes planning and attention. You need to have your materials at hand, dry kindling, and a succession of materials appropriate. Going right from the kindling to a large log won‚Äôt usually work very well. You might get a lot of flame, but it will quickly burn away and go out. To make this work you need to feed the fire with larger and larger twigs and then sticks. Once these are going, you can carefully add the larger logs. All along you need to carefully fan the flames to get it going. When the larger logs start to burn, you can then relax a bit, the fire will take care of itself for a while, but it will still need attention.

A new unit is like that fire. In the beginning you need to start small and carefully manage the process. You can get a big burst of activity only to have it quickly fade. The trick is to keep it going with planning. Bringing on more youth and leaders will sustain the unit. You can then ‚Äúrelax‚Äù a little, but make sure your unit is getting the attention it needs or your unit will fade, just like that fire.

Think longer term. Plan out your entire scouting year in advance. You can always adjust as things change, but having a framework to work from will help keep your unit healthy and on track. If you need help, contact your unit commissioner. He or she can give you advice on how to keep the ‚Äúfire‚Äù going!

Webelos Woods, November 4-6, 2022

Webelos Woods is an awesome opportunity for Webelos (and their parents) to experience some of the fun that they can look forward to once they cross over into a troop. Troops will host Webelos Scouts from various packs and will form patrols which will include those Webelos Scouts for the weekend. Packs will be assigned to camp with a host Troop by the event staff.

On Saturday, the patrols will rotate in a round-robin style of activities where they can visit with different Troops. Each patrol will learn a variety of different outdoor and Scout skills and put them to use in live demonstrations. On Saturday night, both districts will come together to enjoy a rousing campfire program with yells and skits! There will also be a OA Brotherhood ceremony on Saturday evening.

Register today, early bird discount until 10/9/22. Limited space.

Training

Youth Protection training is only valid for two years. Every registered adult must have a current card. Don't wait until the last minute to complete is very necessary task. my.scouting.org

In addition to YPT every leader must be position trained. There are specific training programs for each of the many positions in scouting.

Beyond position training, Hazardous weather training is also required, so don't overlook this as well.

We have in-person training opportunities coming including Adult Leader Training (ALT) for scouts BSA, Introduction to Outdoor Leader Skills IOLS for Scouts BSA and Basic Adult Leader Outdoor Orientation for Cub Scouts and Webelos.

Annual Re-Charter

Every unit must re-charter their unit each year. This involves several steps. You can start working on this now.

  • Update rosters both youth and adult.
  • Review training status for every adult. Current Youth Protection is required.
  • Collect adult and scout applications if required.
  • Collect fees

Start now and this process will be much easier. Wait until the last minute and it is hard on everyone.
